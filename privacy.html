Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 29, 2026

Trimly ("we", "us", "our") is a weight loss planning tool. This policy explains what data we collect, why, and your rights over it. We keep things simple: your data is yours, we don't sell it, and you can delete it any time.

1. What We Collect

Account data. If you create an account, we store your email address to identify you and send sign-in links. No password is ever stored.

Health data. When you log weekly check-ins, we store your weight entries (in pounds), optional notes, and dates. This is health-related data and we treat it with extra care.

Plan data. Your calculator inputs (current weight, goal weight, height, age, activity level, calorie targets) are saved so your plan persists across sessions.

Analytics. If you consent to cookies, we use PostHog to collect anonymous usage events — things like "app opened", "check-in added", "milestone reached". This data is linked to a random device ID, not your name or email, until you sign in.

Device ID. We generate a random anonymous ID stored in your browser (localStorage) so we can understand how the app is used before sign-in. It is never shared with third parties.

We never collect or infer your race, religion, political views, or anything unrelated to weight tracking. We never sell your data to advertisers or data brokers.

2. How We Use It

  • Provide and sync your plan and check-in history across devices
  • Send sign-in links to your email when you request them
  • Understand how the app is used so we can improve it (analytics, with your consent)
  • Track streaks, milestones, and progress within the app

3. Who We Share It With

Supabase — our database and authentication provider (supabase.com). Your email and weight data are stored encrypted in their cloud infrastructure (US region). Supabase is GDPR-compliant and SOC 2 Type II certified.

PostHog — our analytics provider (posthog.com), used only if you accept cookies. They receive anonymous usage events and your device ID. No weight data or email is sent to PostHog.

No other third parties receive your data.

4. Cookies & Local Storage

We use browser localStorage (not cookies) to save your plan and check-ins locally on your device. This data never leaves your device unless you create an account.

If you accept analytics, PostHog sets cookies to track anonymous sessions. You can decline these at any time — the app works fully without them.

5. Data Retention

Your account and data are retained until you request deletion. Anonymous device IDs and analytics data are retained for up to 2 years by PostHog.

6. Your Rights

Depending on where you live (EU, UK, California, etc.) you have some or all of these rights:

  • Access — request a copy of your data
  • Correction — correct inaccurate data (you can edit check-ins directly in the app)
  • Deletion — ask us to delete your account and all associated data
  • Portability — export your check-in history from the app at any time
  • Opt-out of analytics — decline cookies in the consent banner, or contact us to opt out entirely

To exercise any right, email us at beaudfoster@gmail.com. We'll respond within 30 days.

7. GDPR (EU / UK Residents)

Weight data is considered a special category of health data under GDPR. We process it under Article 9(2)(a) — your explicit consent, given when you enter data into the app.

Our lawful bases: contract (providing the service you signed up for) and consent (analytics cookies). You can withdraw consent any time.

Our data processor agreements with Supabase and PostHog meet GDPR Article 28 requirements.

8. CCPA (California Residents)

We do not sell personal information as defined by the CCPA. You have the right to know what data we hold about you and to request its deletion. Contact us at the address below.

9. Children

Trimly is not intended for users under 16. We do not knowingly collect data from children. If you believe a child has provided us data, please contact us and we'll delete it promptly.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy as the app evolves. We'll update the "last updated" date above. For significant changes, we'll notify signed-in users by email.
